Gen Z & Banking

Why it is Important

Generation Z represents the largest and most influential demographic shaping the future of financial services. With $143 billion in buying power in the US alone (nearly 40% of consumer sales) [139], and by 2030 comprising 30% of the workforce with ~$12 trillion in spending power [140], Gen Z’s expectations around digital, transparency, and values-driven finance are resetting the bar. 96% of Gen Z use online banking [139], yet 32% still plan to bank in-branch in 2025 [141], underscoring the need for hybrid service models.

Market Size / Opportunity

Global Spending Power: ~$450B globally; $360B in the US [142].
Future Wealth: Gen Z expected to hold $33T in assets by 2025 and be 27% of the global population [143].
Primary Institution Mix: 79% choose large banks [144], yet 52% are willing to switch to community banks [145].

Important Insights

Digital-first, branch-appreciating: 43% of Gen Z say physical branches are important for “peace of mind” [146].
Investment behavior: Only 66% use traditional brokerages vs 92% of Boomers; 23% use only fintech for investments [142].
Risk tolerance: 36% would give up bank accounts for cryptocurrency; traditional brands still rank high in consideration [147].

Success Stories

  • Bank of America Mobile Innovation: Early rollout of biometrics (Touch ID, etc.) tailored to Gen Z preferences [148].
  • Revolut’s Gen Z Strategy: Investments, crypto trading, and social finance features built for digital natives [149].
  • Chase & Capital One Brand Strength: Traditional banks retain strong mindshare among Gen Z [147].
  • Cash App Penetration: P2P products with broad Gen Z usage and engagement [139].
  • Chime Adoption: High Gen Z adoption among neobanks, underscoring appetite for digital-first experiences [144].

Ideas for CEE Banks

  • Social Impact Banking Platform: Tie spending and investing to verifiable social/environmental outcomes with transparent impact tracking.
  • Gamified Financial Education: Level-based learning, peer competitions, and rewards to build healthy money habits.
  • Creator Economy Banking: Income smoothing, expense tracking, and IP-backed financing for creators and freelancers.
  • Peer-to-Peer Financial Planning: Social goal-setting, collaborative investing, and community-driven advice.
  • Micro-Investment Ecosystem: Auto-invest rounding into sustainable funds, local businesses, and P2P lending with social sharing.
