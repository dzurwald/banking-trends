Financial Inclusion

Why it is Important

Financial inclusion tackles a massive challenge: half of the world’s adults don’t use formal financial services and 75% of poor people are unbanked [162]. The spread of mobile phones and internet connectivity lets institutions reach underserved populations more easily [163]. Meanwhile, government programs and regulations are creating supportive frameworks for inclusion (e.g., digital ID and national strategies) [166].

Market Size / Opportunity

Microfinance: $310.10B in 2025 growing to $797.11B by 2034 (11.06% CAGR) [164].
Inclusive Finance: $1.3B (2024) to $3.95B by 2033 at 13% CAGR [165].

Important Insights

Mobile‑First: Digital wallets and mobile money bridge access gaps where branches are scarce [163].
Government Support: National strategies + digital identity accelerate inclusion and KYC compliance [166].
SME Focus: Tailored microfinance and micro‑insurance products unlock growth for small businesses [164].

Success Stories

  • RBI (India) Inclusion Campaign – Onboarded millions of rural residents into basic banking through targeted initiatives [167].
  • Kenya e‑FinEdu (via SMS) – Free financial education to 3M+ subscribers, boosting inclusion via mobile channels [167].
  • Accion Digital Investment – $152.5M to help MSMEs join the digital economy worldwide [164].
  • Mobile Money Services – Rapid adoption in developing countries narrows the banked–unbanked divide [163].
  • Micro‑insurance – Tailored protection products extend safety nets to underserved populations [163].
