Wealth Distribution & The $84 Trillion Great Wealth Transfer

Why it is Important

The Great Wealth Transfer represents the largest intergenerational wealth movement in human history, with $84 trillion expected to transfer from Baby Boomers to younger generations by 2045 [49][50][51]. This creates both massive opportunities and existential risks for traditional banks, as 87% of wealth inheritors plan to move their assets to different financial institutions [50].

The transfer coincides with the emergence of programmable money and smart contracts, enabling new approaches to inheritance planning, estate management, and intergenerational wealth coordination [52][53].

The receiving generations—Millennials and Gen Z—differ vastly from prior generations, favoring ESG investments, cryptocurrency, and digital-first solutions over traditional products [55][54].

Market Size / Opportunity

The global wealth management market reached $1,636.83 billion in 2024 and is projected to grow to $4,893.17 billion by 2034—a 10.6% CAGR [56].

Of the total $84 trillion transfer volume, $35.8 trillion (42%) will come from high-net-worth and ultra-high-net-worth households—just 1.5% of all households [51].

Programmable money and smart contracts—enabling conditional asset transfers and automated estate planning—are revolutionizing wealth transfer mechanisms [52][57].

Important Insights

Smart contracts simplify inheritance proceedings, handle multi-jurisdictional regulations, and ensure transparent plan execution based on preset conditions [52][58].

A dual-market imperative exists: banks must serve aging wealthy clients with traditional needs while also appealing to digital-first inheritors—requiring customized, generation-spanning value propositions [59][50].
