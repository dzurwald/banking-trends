Wealth Distribution & The $84 Trillion Great Wealth Transfer

Why it is Important

The Great Wealth Transfer represents the largest intergenerational wealth movement in history, with $84 trillion expected to move from Baby Boomers to younger generations by 2045 [49][50][51]. Critically, 87% of inheritors plan to move assets to different financial institutions [50]. This wave coincides with the rise of programmable money and smart contracts, unlocking new possibilities in inheritance planning and estate management [52][53]. Younger generations prioritize ESG, liquidity, alt-investments, and digital experiences over traditional financial approaches [55][54].

Market Size / Opportunity

The global wealth management market was $1,636.8B in 2024 and is projected to grow to $4,893.2B by 2034 (10.6% CAGR) [56]. Around $35.8T (42%) of the transfer comes from HNW/UHNW households, representing just 1.5% of households [51]. Innovative tools like programmable money are revolutionizing estate distribution via conditional transfers and smart contracts [52][57].

Important Insights

Smart contracts are transforming inheritance by handling multi-jurisdiction laws, conditional fund release, and transparent estate planning [52][58]. The challenge and opportunity lie in serving both traditional clients and their tech-savvy heirs with differentiated, digital-first wealth solutions [59][50].

Success Stories

  • BlackRock’s Digital Estate Planning: Smart contract–enabled inheritance, overseeing $21T+ in assets.
  • Vanguard’s Intergenerational Wealth Services: Hybrid advisor + digital tools, managing $250B+ across clients and their heirs.
  • Charles Schwab’s Programmable Wealth Transfer: Automated rebalancing and conditional asset release via smart contracts.
  • Circle’s Programmable Money for Estates: USDC-powered inheritance planning with conditional execution.
  • DBS Digital Estate Transformation: AI-enhanced estate planning integrated with smart contract inheritances—driving growth in wealth management service adoption.
