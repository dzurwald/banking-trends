Longevity Economy & Aging Population Banking

Why it is Important

The longevity economy marks a major demographic shift: the global population aged 60+ is expected to reach 2.1 billion by 2050—nearly double current levels [62]. This isn’t just about aging—it’s about active aging, lifelong learning, healthcare integration, and flexible financial tools like reverse mortgages [63][64]. The economic impact is enormous: the Silver Economy made up 34% of global GDP ($45T) in 2022 and is projected to reach $118T (39%) by 2050 [65].

Market Size / Opportunity

Aging population services are projected to reach $1,754.9B by 2030 (5.7% CAGR) [66]. Wealth is heavily concentrated: in the US, adults above 50 control 53.2% of total wealth, about $62.1 trillion in assets [67]. Key service areas include health financing, long-term care, and flexible mortgage products like equity release and reverse mortgages [64].

Important Insights

Seniors face extended retirement periods (30+ years), soaring healthcare costs, and the need for flexible income solutions [62][68]. Reverse mortgages and hybrid financing models are becoming critical for maintaining quality of life and preserving intergenerational wealth [64]. Digital inclusion is also vital—banks serving seniors well blend intuitive design with human support to bridge the tech gap [69][70][67].

Success Stories

  • DBS Golden Circle Program: Healthcare financing, reverse mortgages, requalification support, and higher yields for seniors [71].
  • ANZ Longevity Platform: Health-finance integration with flexible equity releases and user-friendly interfaces.
  • ICICI Senior Solutions: Automated medical expense management, insurance, and financing for lifelong learning.
  • Commonwealth Bank Retirement Ecosystem: Hybrid platform combining reverse equity, health financing, and career transition tools.
  • Nationwide Later Life Lending: Age-friendly mortgages including lifetime and retirement interest-only products.
