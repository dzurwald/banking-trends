Security & Digital Identity (Banking)

Why it is Important

Digital identity has evolved from a security feature to critical business infrastructure in banking. Every banking interaction – from account opening to payment authorization – depends on robust identity verification and authentication [150]. The rising threat landscape makes this even more crucial, with an 86% identity-based attack rate facing banks [151]. Digital identity in banking is enabling seamless onboarding with video identification and document verification, reducing account opening time from days to minutes [150], while simultaneously strengthening security postures against sophisticated cyber threats.

Important Insights

Regulatory Transformation: eIDAS 2 (effective since May 2024) creates an interoperable framework for secure digital identities across EU member states, enabling banks to leverage a pan‑European identity ecosystem [150].
Biometric Adoption Accelerating: By 2027, 92% of tier‑one banks globally will implement at least three distinct biometric authentication methods [155]. 40% of banks now use physical biometrics to fight fraud, up from 26% five years ago [154].
Mobile‑First Evolution: Global biometric smartphone users reached 471 million, with 30% of mobile devices using biometric authentication, accelerating bank adoption of mobile biometrics [148].
