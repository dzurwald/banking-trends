Monetisation of Internal Capabilities

Why it is Important

The monetization of internal capabilities represents a paradigm shift where banks transform IT from a cost center into a revenue engine. Banks can increase revenue 1–2% through data monetization—for large institutions, that’s billions in new income streams [156]. This leverages unique data assets, tech infrastructure, and regulatory expertise to create new product lines. Revolut’s People platform is a showcase: it began as an internal HR tool and evolved into a comprehensive B2B product [157][158], proving how in-house capabilities can become external revenue streams.

Market Size / Opportunity

Opportunity at a glance:
– 1–2% revenue uplift potential from banking data monetization [156].
– Passwordless authentication market alone projected to exceed $53B by 2030, illustrating monetizable identity capabilities [154].
– Embedded finance can add up to $70 in annual revenue per customer via fees and retention, when banks productize capabilities for partners [114].

Important Insights

Data-centric transformation: Banks must shift from digital enablement to data monetization, creating internal data marketplaces and external API products [159].
Platform strategy: Revolut People scaled from an internal tool for ~600 staff to a platform supporting 10,000+ employees—then externalized for B2B clients [157].
Regulatory advantage: Existing compliance and risk frameworks give banks a moat when offering capabilities via BaaS/partner channels [160].

Success Stories

  • Revolut People Platform – Internal HR solution evolved into an AI-driven talent platform offered to external clients [157][158].
  • JPMorgan Chase – Enterprise data strategy prioritizing AI and fraud prevention to drive productivity and new value capture [156].
  • European Bank (Data Analytics) – Generated $12M+ annually by selling subscription analytics to external clients [161].
  • Keyrus-led Initiative – Built internal data marketplaces and external API services while staying compliant [159].
  • Asian Bank (Consumer Insights) – Earned $15M annually selling anonymized spending insights to partners and agencies [161].

Ideas for CEE Banks

  • RegTech-as-a-Service: Productize internal compliance/KYC/AML processes as services for fintechs and smaller FIs.
  • Risk Analytics Marketplace: Externalize credit scoring, fraud signals, and portfolio risk models via APIs.
  • Banking-as-a-Service: Offer core banking, cards, and payments rails to partners to launch products fast.
  • AI-Powered Advisory: Package internal ML models for robo-advice and predictive insights for other institutions.
  • Cross-Border Payments Engine: Monetize FX, routing, and compliance tooling for CEE↔EU trade corridors.

Personal Recommendation

We should treat our strongest internal capabilities as products, not just internal functions. Start where banks already lead and where market demand is proven: security, biometrics, digital identity & KYC. Package these as APIs and workflows for SMEs, fintechs, marketplaces, and even public institutions. From there, expand into fraud signals, payments/FX rails, credit & risk models, and anonymized data insights.

Banks can leverage idea management platforms to capture, refine, and prioritize opportunities from across the organization — turning small ideas into commercial offerings. Innovating the business model and finding new revenue streams from existing strengths is essential. These are products we already have; with one small pivot we could scale them. Why not use it?

This approach aligns perfectly with Beyond Banking and horizontal scaling strategies. By monetizing internal capabilities, we diversify income, strengthen partnerships, and position the bank as a trusted infrastructure provider in the digital economy.
